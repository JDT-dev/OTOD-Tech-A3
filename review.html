Personal Reflections

Team Member 1

After the initial confusion around the requirements of this assignment and the delays in receiving clear guidance, I felt somewhat on the backfoot and a little worried about meeting the due date. Luckily for me, my team included four extremely strong and capable members and we came together and planned out the project efficiently and effectively by breaking down the requirements into manageable tasks and assigned each task to match the strengths of each member.

What went well

Our communication was stronger than it was in A2. We had a better understanding of each other and how we worked as team. I was able to plot out the initial plan using a Gantt chart without worrying if the tasks were going to be completed on time. Each member was assigned their sections and we went away and worked on them and provided status checks during each of our weekly meetings.

What could be improved

I understand that there is always room for improvement, but in this case, we worked extremely well together, and I always felt like someone had my back. We all had unexpected life curve balls thrown at us during this project and my team members were there to jump in and pick-up the slack, particularly when again, we lost another team member without notice during week 3 of the project. This left us feeling a little deflated and confused but we came together, allocated the shortfall and got to work on the project. Under the circumstances, I couldn’t have asked for a better team.

At least one thing surprising

How well we continued to work together. By this time, many teams tend to show signs of issues, yes, we lost a member, and we still don’t know the reason, but the rest of us, communicated and kept each other motivated to get the project done.

What also surprised me, was how much effort we all put into supporting the team member that left, and still we received no explanation for why they abandoned the project. This behaviour I find extremely unprofessional, but I also understand that each member deals with their own issues in their own way.


One thing I have learned about groups

That everyone has their own strengths and if you lucky enough to attract the right balance of people and personalities and everyone is dedicated to working hard and producing high standards of work, you can’t fail.

Team Member 2

There is no doubt about it, this is a good team. After many weeks of working together we feel comfortable and trust each other, and I believe everyone felt they were able to have an equal amount of input into the projects we’ve been working on. I genuinely feel like I would like to work on further projects with each team member in the future.

What went well

Continuing on from our A2, OTOD Tech group communicated very well with twice weekly team meetings and catch up’s during the week for clarification on various aspects of the project. I was excited to show the other team members what I had been working on since we last met up and was keen for their input. This goes to show how truly comfortable we’ve become with each other. Also, each team member has a great sense of humour and we all have similar interests, so we always had something to talk about.


What could be improved

I am struggling to find anything that we could improve on in our group. The main issue was when we lost another team member without notice and I believe it was completely out of our hands. We did spend a lot of time reflecting on why another team member would leave, but we all felt like we had reached out to this team member numerous times to try to find out why they left but didn’t have any luck. We all wish this team member well.

At least one thing that was surprising

As we are close to finalising our assignments, I am surprised at how sad I am at not being able to work together as a team in the future. I would like to do some further study in the future but would only really like to continue with this same group. I have found as I started preparing for a solo assignment without my team, that I’m not looking forward to it now as much as I was when I first started this course. I feel like we work better as a group, and I love hearing everyone’s perspective on the different aspects of our assignments.

At least one thing that you have learned about groups

I learned that groups are essential. I would have always believed I worked better alone. It is so much easier not to have to worry about other people, what they’re doing and how they’re completing their tasks. The opposite is true - I found working in a group enhanced my own skills. I learnt so much from each and every team member. If I felt low and couldn’t get motivated, I instantly thought of how hard everyone was working and I didn’t want to let them down, so I tried harder. Working in a group was a huge learning curve and now I cannot imagine working any other way.

Tools & Audit Trail

GitHub

The GitHub audit trail is a true reflection of our teams work efforts. Though the commit history is subjective, as it depends on what the commits represents, i.e. one member making many mistakes or one member adding quality code? Either way, we were all still learning to strengthen our commit processes and our way around GitHub in general. Some team member's were more confident with it but having other members less confident resulted in apprehension and sometimes errors, but it was all a learning curve which we managed to overcome to a point, and eventually found our groove resulting in what we believe to be a very successful website and something we were all proud to be part of.

Do we all now love GitHub after this experience? The jury is still it out.

We found MS Teams the perfect tool to manage and coordinate our team. It provided a Task Planner which we transferred all of our Group Schedule (Gantt Chart) tasks into and assigned each task to members of the team. These were tracked with due dates and followed up during our meetings. Some team members had little experience with Teams but did an amazing job to get up to speed and appreciate the features of the program.

Our Team consisted of the following channels:

  • General
  • GitHub and Website
  • Interesting Leaning Links
  • Meetings
  • Project Brainstorming
  • Project Management

This made it easier to find relevant chat threads and other information relating to the specific area of our project.

Our online meetings were successful and without too many connectivity issues. We were able to easily share our screens, swap control and record. The recordings were easy to store as well as all of the other documents required to complete our project.
