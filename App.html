Why do you need it?

Care your puppy and family. It is obvious that parents need help in training their puppy while balancing their family. Encouraging parents to schedule and focus on the puppy’s needs during the first 16 weeks (“Critical period"), will help create a strong foundation for when the puppy grows into an adult dog. By focusing on the interaction between a puppy and a child, this will help the parent and child realize that they have an affectionate, caring lifetime companion that truly is humanity's best friend.

Our Motivation

We should be responsible.

Research conducted in 2012-2013 discovered that approximately 211,655 dogs end up in pounds, and of that, approximately 43,900 are killed (21%). Dogs are loving, compassionate beings that live in the moment and give nothing but unconditional love. We do not need to be killing so many innocent creatures. It's not the dogs' fault that they end up in that situation. It's ours due to our lack of knowledge around what a puppy needs early on in their life.

That's our motivation, to equip families with the knowledge to prevent future deaths of dogs because of the lack of socialisation and training received during their formative years.
