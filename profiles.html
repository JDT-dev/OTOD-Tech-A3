Group processes

Looking back at the group work we did together in Assignment 2 it is easy to understand why the phrase “hindsight is a beautiful thing” is so often used. As our first group assignment I believe we worked well together but we were not without our problems. It’s not easy to co-ordinate an assignment and all of its complex parts. Bringing together a group of people who have not worked together before and who also had various time limits to commit to the assignment due to work and life balances, sometimes proved challenging.

Assignment 2 was a comprehensive project that required research, particularly into technologies we had no prior knowledge of, and we needed to have it delivered within a specified time frame. We were all committed to doing it to the best of our ability. Some in our group were available to brainstorm our project during the day and some were only available quite late in the evening and even then, had limited time due to responsibilities with their families. This resulted in some members only seeing the progress of the group a few days after the others had already completed their part of the project. In hindsight, we should have made allowances for the availability of time for some of our team members and thought about how their tasks could be completed given their time limits.

Moving on to Assignment 3, I expect it to be a much easier process now that we've gotten to know each other and have worked together so well in our first group assignment. We know how to assign tasks and who has the ability and time to do each of the tasks that we have allocated to team members. We’ve tried to make certain that all our team have contributed to each part of the project by pairing up team members and having this rotate in each section of the project so that every member is having input into each part of the Assignment. We have also been able to ascertain the strengths of some team members. Some of our team members are more confident in project management, others like writing, some are very interested in Web page development and others in animation and video editing. We are communicating well as a team and are confident in our ability to complete this project together and at a very high standard.

  • Programmers

Programmers create, test, and maintain software that meets industry standards for performance, reliability, and security. Programmers may work on a variety of projects, including designing mobile apps, coding video games, and creating websites.

  • Computer Systems Analyst

Computer systems analyst experts are the multitaskers of information technology. They must understand computer hardware, software, and networks and how they all come to work together. Their job requires a multitude of different skills, including the ability to analyse information quickly and effectively, excellent communication, and the ability to persuade when necessary.

Graphic Designers / Desktop publishers develop visual concepts that inspire, educate, and captivate customers, either through computer software or by hand. They create the overall layout and production design for mobile applications, webpages, advertising, brochures and magazines, amongst other things.

Group communication

OTOD Tech have communicated using Microsoft Teams with twice weekly recorded video meetings and all files uploaded to the Files channels in the Teams App. We have used a group Word template document in Teams that everyone has access to as a draft for our Assignment with everyone working on sections independently and then uploading to this document for other Team members to review.

All team members are required to attend every meeting. If they cannot attend a meeting or need a meeting time to be rescheduled, they must let everyone know prior to the meeting so that another time can be agreed upon. If a team member does not attend a meeting or becomes inactive, all team members will reach out to them to check if all is well and if the team member doesn’t respond, we will then email our Course instructors to let them know that a team member has gone missing and ask for an update on the activity of the inactive team member.

We have found this to be the perfect method of communication for our Team.
